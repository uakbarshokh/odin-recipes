Classic Shakshuka with Feta

Description

A North African and Middle Eastern staple, Shakshuka is a one-pan dish of eggs gently poached in a simmering, spiced tomato and bell pepper sauce. It's completely vegetarian and perfect for any time of day.

Ingredients

  • 4-5 large eggs
  • 1 large can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 red bell pepper (diced)
  • 1 yellow onion (diced)
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • 1/4 tsp chili powder (optional)
  • 1/3 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Serve with: Crusty bread or pita for dipping.

Steps

  1. Sauté vegetables: Heat olive oil in a large cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and bell pepper, cooking until soft (about 5 minutes). Add the garlic and spices, cooking for 1 more minute.
  2. Simmer sauce: Pour in the crushed tomatoes. Season with salt and pepper. Let the sauce simmer uncovered for 10-15 minutes so it reduces and thickens slightly.
  3. Poach eggs: Use a spoon to make small wells in the sauce. Crack an egg directly into each well.
  4. Cover and cook: Cover the skillet with a lid and cook for 5-8 minutes, or until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny.
  5. Garnish: Remove from heat, sprinkle generously with crumbled feta cheese and fresh cilantro. Serve immediately with warm bread.
