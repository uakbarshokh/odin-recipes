Khoresh Gheymeh

Description

This is a traditional Persian stew usually made with lamb or beef, but chicken thighs make it incredibly tender and quick to cook. It's rich, aromatic, and deeply comforting when served over steamed basmati rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 1 cup yellow split peas (rinsed)
  • 1 large onion (finely diced)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 2 dried Persian limes (pierced with a fork) or 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 3 cups chicken broth or water
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Serve with: Steamed basmati rice and topped with crispy shoestring potato fries (traditional).

Steps

  1. Sauté: Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onions and sauté until golden and soft (about 7-10 minutes).
  2. Spice it up: Stir in the turmeric and cinnamon, cooking for 1 minute until fragrant. Add the chicken pieces and brown them on all sides.
  3. Build the base: Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes until it darkens slightly.
  4. Simmer: Add the rinsed yellow split peas, dried limes (or lemon juice), and broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 45-60 minutes until the split peas are tender but not mushy, and the chicken is cooked through.
  5. Serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot over basmati rice, topped with a handful of crispy potato sticks.
